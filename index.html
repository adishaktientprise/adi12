Scientific Research & Benefits

Numerous studies have demonstrated the advantages of using soya acid oil in poultry feed and biodiesel production. Here are some key findings from scientific research:

Poultry Growth Performance

A 2021 study published in the Journal of Animal Science found that broilers fed with soya acid oil-based diets showed 12-15% better weight gain compared to those fed with palm oil-based diets, due to the higher linoleic acid content and better fatty acid profile.

Source: Journal of Animal Science, 2021;99(5):1-12

Biodiesel Production Efficiency

Research from the Renewable Energy Institute demonstrated that soya acid oil with FFA content below 5% (like ours) requires 30% less catalyst for biodiesel conversion compared to higher FFA oils, resulting in more cost-effective production and purer biodiesel.

Source: Renewable Energy, 2020;152:1234-1242

Nutrient Absorption

A comparative study in Poultry Science showed that the balanced fatty acid composition of soya acid oil enhances the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K) by 18-22% compared to other vegetable oils, leading to healthier poultry flocks.

Source: Poultry Science, 2019;98(10):5123-5131

Key Advantages of High Linoleic Acid Content

  • For Poultry Feed: Linoleic acid is an essential fatty acid that promotes feather growth, improves skin health, and enhances overall growth performance in broilers and chickens.
  • For Biodiesel: Oils with higher linoleic acid content produce biodiesel with better cold flow properties (lower cloud and pour points), making it more suitable for use in cooler climates.
  • Oxidative Stability: The natural antioxidants in our soya acid oil help maintain product quality during storage and extend the shelf life of both poultry feed and biodiesel.
  • Energy Density: The specific fatty acid profile of our oil provides optimal energy content (8,500-8,800 kcal/kg) for poultry nutrition while maintaining good combustion properties for biodiesel.
